What is the difference between Margin, Border, and Padding?

Borders, margins and padding are, put simply, CSS styling that allows developers to move and position different parts (or elements) of a web page. Borders define the edges, margins create space between elements, and padding ensures content within elements is neatly framed and not too close or far from the border.

Borders

The border is the line that wraps around an element. It can be styled in terms of width, colour and style. Below is an example of an element with a border that is 3 pixels wide, coloured green with dashed lines as the style of border.

Padding

Padding is the space between the border and the content of the element. It increases the inner space of the element. The first image has padding of 5px and the second image has padding of 15 pixels, moving the border further away from the element.

Margin

Margin is the space outside the border. It creates distance between different elements. This is helpful to set space between two elements on a page, for example you might want some space between two images.

In real terms

A helpful analogy I came across was thinking of a picture in a frame hung on the wall. The photo itself is the element. The space between the photo and the frame is the padding. The frame is the border and the space between the edge of the frame and the next photo hanging on the wall is margin.
